# CO₂ and Temperature Over 800,000 Years

The ice core record shows **atmospheric CO₂** and **temperature** over the past 800,000 years.

> **Key Question:** What do you notice about the relationship between temperature and atmospheric CO₂?

<div class="key-idea">

There is a strong **positive correlation**: when CO₂ is high, temperature tends to be high. When CO₂ is low, temperature tends to be low.

</div>

<div class="warning">

**Correlation ≠ Causation** (yet!) — We observe a pattern, but we need a mechanism to explain it.

</div>

# Rates of Change: Then vs. Now

### Natural Rate of CO₂ Change

190 ppm → 280 ppm over ~7,000 years
**Rate ≈ 0.013 ppm/year**

### Modern Rate of CO₂ Change

280 ppm → 421 ppm over ~200 years
**Rate ≈ 0.705 ppm/year**

<div class="warning">

The modern rate of CO₂ increase is **~50–80 times faster** than the natural rate of change!

</div>

<!--

# The Greenhouse Effect: PhET Simulation

### What happens when greenhouse gas levels change?

| GHG Level | Sunlight (Yellow) | Infrared (Red) | Temperature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **None** | Passes through | Escapes to space | Very low (~−18°C) |
| **Low** | Same | Some absorbed, re-emitted | Slight increase |
| **Moderate** | Same | More trapped, bouncing | Further increase |
| **High** | Same | Many trapped, cycling | Significant increase |

<div class="key-idea">

Greenhouse gases don't block sunlight — they **trap infrared radiation** (heat) that Earth's surface emits after absorbing sunlight.

</div>

<!--
TEACHER MOVE: Have students use the PhET Greenhouse Effect simulation ("Waves" tab). First set GHG to "none" and start sunlight. Then slowly increase.
CONFERRING QUESTIONS: "Where is the energy coming from? How does the energy change form when it hits Earth's surface? How does the energy react as greenhouse gas levels rise?"
KEY POINTS TO SURFACE: Sunlight → absorbed by surface → re-emitted as infrared → trapped by GHG molecules → re-emitted toward surface → warming. This is the MECHANISM.
COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS: Students may think GHGs block sunlight (they don't — they trap heat). Some may think heat is "created" — it's just retained longer.
TIMING: ~20-25 minutes for simulation exploration.
-->

# The Greenhouse Effect — How It Works

<div class="columns">
<div class="col">

**The Mechanism:**

1. Sunlight reaches Earth as **visible light**
2. Some is **reflected** by clouds/ice
3. The rest is **absorbed** by Earth's surface
4. Surface re-emits energy as **infrared radiation** (heat)
5. Greenhouse gases **absorb and re-emit** infrared
6. Heat is **trapped** near the surface

</div>
<div class="col">

**Without greenhouse gases:**
Temperature ≈ −18°C (0°F)

**With natural greenhouse gases:**
Temperature ≈ 15°C (59°F)

**With enhanced greenhouse gases:**
Temperature is **rising** above natural levels

</div>
</div>

<!--
TEACHER MOVE: Students should have read The Greenhouse Effect text. Review the mechanism. Clarify the analogy: like a puffy coat trapping body heat, or a hot car — sunlight enters, is converted to heat, and is trapped.
KEY POINTS TO SURFACE: The greenhouse effect is not inherently bad — without it, Earth would be frozen. The ENHANCED greenhouse effect from extra CO₂ is the problem.
COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS: "Greenhouse effect" ≠ "bad." Students may confuse the natural greenhouse effect (good) with the enhanced greenhouse effect (problematic).
TIMING: ~10 minutes for review and discussion.
-->

# Summary: Conservation in Earth's Systems

<div class="columns">
<div class="col">

### Matter is Conserved

Carbon is **not created or destroyed** — it moves between reservoirs. Burning fossil fuels transfers carbon from the geosphere to the atmosphere. The total remains the same.

</div>
<div class="col">

### Energy is Conserved

Energy from the Sun is **not created or destroyed** — it changes form (light → heat) and moves between systems. Greenhouse gases delay its release to space, temporarily storing more energy in the atmosphere.

</div>
</div>

<div class="key-idea">

**Correlation → Causation:** The rate of CO₂ increase matches the timeline of fossil fuel burning. The greenhouse effect provides the **causal mechanism**. Together, these establish causation, not just correlation.

</div>

<!--

# Experiment: Surface Color and Temperature

<div class="columns">
<div class="col">

- **Controlled:** Jar size, distance from lamp, starting temp

</div>
</div>

</div>

# The Albedo Effect

<div class="vocab">

**Albedo** — The fraction of incoming solar radiation that a surface reflects. Ranges from 0 (absorbs all) to 1 (reflects all).

</div>

<div class="columns">
<div class="col">

**High Albedo Surfaces:**
- Fresh snow: ~0.80–0.90
- Sea ice: ~0.50–0.70
- Clouds: ~0.60–0.90

*Reflect most sunlight*

</div>
<div class="col">

**Low Albedo Surfaces:**
- Open ocean: ~0.06
- Dark soil: ~0.10
- Forest: ~0.10–0.20

*Absorb most sunlight*

</div>
</div>

<div class="key-idea">

When ice melts, it is replaced by ocean or land with **much lower albedo**, causing that area to absorb dramatically more solar energy.

</div>

<!--
TEACHER MOVE: Introduce the term "albedo" formally. Show the PBS LearningMedia resource (Introduction and Ice-Albedo Feedback tabs). Ask students to describe the albedo effect in their own words.

TIMING: ~10-15 minutes for introduction and discussion.

# The Ice-Albedo Positive Feedback Loop

### Cause and Effect Sequence:

1. 🔥 **Global temperatures increase** (enhanced greenhouse effect)
2. 🧊 **Arctic ice melts** → darker ocean/land exposed
3. ☀️ **More solar radiation absorbed** (lower albedo)
4. 🌡️ **Local temperatures increase further**
5. 🔄 **More ice melts** → return to step 2

<div class="warning">

This is a **positive feedback loop** — the output reinforces the input, **amplifying** the original change rather than dampening it.

</div>

<!--
TEACHER MOVE: Students work with card sort pieces to build this cause-and-effect sequence. Then individually summarize in their handout. Select 2-3 groups to share during Class Consensus Discussion.
EXPECTED STUDENT RESPONSES: Students should construct the loop showing warming → melting → less reflection → more warming → more melting.
CCC CONNECTION: #7 Stability and Change — positive feedback loops DESTABILIZE systems. If ice expands, more radiation reflects → cooling → more ice (and vice versa). The system amplifies changes.
CONFERRING QUESTIONS: "What would happen if temperatures decreased instead? Would the loop work in reverse?"
TIMING: ~20-25 minutes for card sort and individual writing.
-->

# Explaining Arctic Amplification

> **Why is the Arctic warming ~4 times faster than the rest of the planet?**

The complete explanation:

1. **1800s:** Industrial Revolution → burning fossil fuels → CO₂ rises ~50–80x faster than natural
2. **Enhanced greenhouse effect:** Extra CO₂ traps more infrared radiation → global warming
3. **Arctic ice melts:** Warming causes sea ice and glaciers to shrink
4. **Albedo decreases:** Dark ocean/land replaces reflective ice
5. **More heat absorbed:** Lower albedo → more solar energy retained locally
6. **Positive feedback:** More warming → more melting → even lower albedo → accelerating warming

<div class="key-idea">

The Arctic has **more ice to lose** than any other region, making the ice-albedo feedback loop especially powerful there.

</div>

# How Temperature Affects the Carbon Cycle

| System Interaction | Temps Stable | Temps Increasing ↑ | Temps Decreasing ↓ |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ocean ↔ Atmosphere** | CO₂ exchanges balanced | Ocean releases more CO₂ (holds less when warm) | Ocean absorbs more CO₂ (holds more when cool) |
| **Biosphere ↔ Atmosphere** | Photosynthesis ≈ respiration | More respiration & decay → more CO₂ released | Less respiration & decay → less CO₂ released |
| **Cryosphere ↔ Atmosphere** | Carbon frozen in permafrost | Permafrost thaws → bacteria release CO₂ & methane | Permafrost freezes → carbon stays trapped |

<div class="key-idea">

When temperatures increase → CO₂ **increases** in the atmosphere (from all three sources). This is the **greenhouse gas feedback loop**.

</div>

# Key Vocabulary Reference

<div class="vocab">

**Greenhouse Effect** — The process by which greenhouse gases in the atmosphere absorb and re-emit infrared radiation, trapping heat near Earth's surface

**Enhanced Greenhouse Effect** — The additional warming caused by human-emitted greenhouse gases above natural levels

**Albedo** — The fraction of incoming solar radiation reflected by a surface (0 = absorbs all, 1 = reflects all)

**Positive Feedback Loop** — A cycle in which the output of a process reinforces and amplifies the original change

**Carbon Reservoir** — A component of the Earth system that stores carbon (atmosphere, ocean, biosphere, geosphere)

**Carbon Cycle** — The movement of carbon between reservoirs through natural and human-driven processes

</div>

# Key Vocabulary Reference (continued)

<div class="vocab">

**Rate of Change** — How quickly a variable changes over time; calculated as (change in value) ÷ (change in time)

**Correlation** — A statistical relationship between two variables (they change together) — does not prove one causes the other

**Causation** — A relationship where one variable directly causes a change in another, supported by a mechanism and empirical evidence

**Feedback Mechanism** — A process where the output of a system influences its own input, either amplifying (positive) or dampening (negative) change

**Arctic Amplification** — The phenomenon where the Arctic warms faster than the global average due to positive feedback loops, especially ice-albedo feedback

**Permafrost** — Permanently frozen ground that stores large amounts of carbon; thawing releases greenhouse gases

</div>
